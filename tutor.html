Geometry Gauntlet

While I was a senior in high school I helped start a tutoring business with some friends. There was one geometry student that had gone through 3 other tutors during the semester; the parents put in place a pass-guarantee requirement that the tutors had not lived up to. I personally took over the assignment and created a schedule that was realistic and agreeable to those involved. After plenty of exploration and fun, the student was able to achieve a passing grade consistently and obtain a true understanding of geometric principles.

From Despair to Gold

At my high school in West Texas, nine students were chosen in total to partake in a regional academic competition: 3 A-students, 3 B-students, and 3 C-students. Part of the competition was a mathematics exam covering topics from pre-algebra through calculus. I was asked by one of the teachers to help one of the C-students who was having trouble preparing for the mathematics section of the competition. Starting from the ground up, I went through the topics and explained the concepts and helped him explore the material. The student went on to win first place in the C-student category out of all West Texas schools and eventually became successful in the field of economics.

As someone who grew-up with dyslexia, I understand the need for some students to look at concepts from different perspectives to build a strong understanding of the material.
