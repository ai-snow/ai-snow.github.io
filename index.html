Languages help us tell our stories; past, present, and future.

Math is no exception to this and people may be surprised at how profound the stories told through mathematics can be.

Take the following equation from conditional probability:

$$ \text{Probability of A and B} = \left(\text{Probability of A Given B}\right)*\left(\text{Probability of B}\right) $$ $$ P(A\cap B)=P(A|B)P(B) $$

\(A\) and \(B\) can be replaced by any statements, no exceptions.

Let us say \(A\) is the event known as success and \(B\) is the event known as trying.

$$ \text{Probability of trying and success}=\left(\text{Probability of success Given trying}\right)*\left(\text{Probability of trying}\right) $$

Notice that rearranging \(A\) and \(B\) on the left-hand side of the equation does not change the validity of the statement.

In English, what does this equation tell us? Well, break it down:

Probability of trying and succeeding is a measure of self-driven success.

Probability of succeeding given that one tries is a measure of ability.

Probability of trying is a measure of motivation.

We can then write the equation equivalently in English as:

"Self-driven success is the product of ability and motivation"
